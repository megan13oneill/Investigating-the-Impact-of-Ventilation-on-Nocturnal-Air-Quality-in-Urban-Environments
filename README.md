## Outline of the project - 
Project Title: ‘Investigating the Impact of Ventilation on Nocturnal Air Quality in Urban Environments’

Location:
• Bedroom of a high-rise flat in Liverpool city centre 

Question: 
How does leaving a bedroom window open or closed affect indoor air quality, humidity, and temperature during nighttime in an urban setting?

Approach:
Measure CO2 levels/ppm, humidity / %, and temperature / ° C over an 8-hour sleep window at regular intervals. To improve reliability, the measurements will be done over a few days. 

Independent variable: Window open or window closed

Dependant variables:  CO2 levels / ppm, humidity / %, and temperature /  ° C

Control variables: time / s (the time we start measuring and the intervals at which we measure. The same people will be in the room each night. 

Factors that cannot be controlled: Outside weather conditions such as temperature and humidity. As we are measuring the change in these variables, this shouldn’t impact the investigation too much, although it may be more significant on nights when the window is left open. 

Output: To understand nocturnal air quality emissions and the potential implications this may have on health factors such as quality of sleep. To suggest viable solutions to any discoveries uncovered throughout the investigation. 

Data Structure and Visualisation:
Aims: The data acquired from the sensor must be readable and interpretable to ensure the experiment satisfies the FAIR data guidelines.

• Collect data every second over 6 hours between 11 pm to 7 am.  

• What parameters should we be measuring:
	○ Timestamp (ISO 8601 format)
	○ Indoor Air Quality Index (IAQ)
	○ PM2.5 levels (µg/m³)
	○  CO2 levels (ppm)
	○  Humidity (%)
	○  Temperature (°C)

• metadata will be put into the SQL database and tables. 

• Data Analysis:  
	○ Comparative Analysis: Analyze the data to compare indoor air quality metrics between the two conditions.
	○ Visualization: Create graphs to illustrate changes in air quality, humidity, and temperature between nights. Graphs should be accessible and display data clearly and accurately.

• Monitoring: The monitoring of the data should highlight any differences between the two conditions and follow up on any noticeable observations.
